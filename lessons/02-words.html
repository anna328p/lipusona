Content words

Most words in toki pona are content words. Content words are words that have a meaning and aren’t just to provide the structure of the language. Content words can act as nouns, adjectives, or verbs depending on context. In this lesson, we’ll look at using them as nouns.

li

A basic sentence in toki pona is structured like this:

[x1] li [x2].

The word li marks the subject of the sentence. This type of sentence means “[x1] is [x2]”.

ijo li ijo.
Something is something.

jan li pona.
The person is good.

Unlike some other languages, toki pona does not use articles. There are no words like “a” or “the”.

toki pona words have many meanings:

ijo li pona.
Something is good.
The thing is simple.
The being is peaceful.

jan li lipu.
The person is flat.

lipu li pona.
The book is good.

Words aren’t marked for number. This means words don’t change depending on whether the subject is singular and plural.

jan li pona.
People are good.

Definitions

Words are usually defined as only one part of speech: as a noun, a verb, or an adjective. However, nearly any content word can be used as any part of speech! Here, I’m using “toki” as a noun.

ni li toki.
This is a language.
This is a story.
